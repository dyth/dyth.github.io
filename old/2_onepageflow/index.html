Artificial Intelligence by Machine Learning

Artificial Intelligence is a mysterious topic which has entranced me from an early age. Having enjoyed using physical and mathematical techniques to model the world around me, I found it difficult to apply the same set of equations to the concept of intelligence, though it is easy to intuitively grok. Recent advances in deep learning and increased funding of related startups has only increased my desire to pursue a career in this field.

Computer Vision is a key component of an agent's ability to perceived the environment. Another useful input is Natural Language Processing. Using inferred data, it is possible to exploit hard-coded rules of the environment to decide the next action to take using a 1960s technique called Good Old Fashioned Artificial Intelligence, or GOFAI. As hard coding every rule is difficult and time-consuming, Machine Learning techniques are used. Currently, the algorithms that achieve the highest success rates are Deep Learning based. However, recent advances such as AlphaGo combine both Deep Learning and Knowledge Engineering to approximate GOFAI.

A tangential idea to create agents is that of Causal Entropic Forces. An agent would simulate as many paths as possible to take in the future, before choosing the one that would generate the greatest number of future states.

All of my thoughts about AI are in one place -- enabling me to quickly scan all the information or remind me of a forgotten idea.

Programming

Programming is (like) magic (and, indeed, it should be taught that way), as it enables a user to manipulate concepts and ideas through a theoretical framework of invocations and representations. Hypotheses and simulations can be created without mess (or fuss), and physically impossible situations can be constructed. With the nascent online community increasingly being interconnected and suffused with data and information, programs empower their users in whichever way they desire, within a digital era where everybody is equal. Less sentimentally, it's always fun to test to destruction!

At the heart of any segment of code is a Turing Machine, an arbitrary mathematical construct which enables computability to be represented. A slightly less abstract version is a Register Machine. Simple Turing Machines which resemble primitive life-forms are One Dimensional Cellular Automata. Two Dimensional Cellular Automata are more complex, and include the famous Game of Life. Increasingly complicated Turing Machines can simulate programs representing Artificial Lifeforms. Beyond that, algorithms can be invented for anthropic and biological simulations.

Descartes and Spinoza in the 21st Century

An essay I wrote during my second-year studies which won a Trinity College Science Essay Prize. In the essay, I discus the meaning of AI and the ways in which it could be created using a historical-philosophical context. Then I introduce the holism versus reductionism dichotomy, and, using this new context, explore the efficiency and safety of the various techniques and why I believe reductionism is becoming increasingly relevant.
